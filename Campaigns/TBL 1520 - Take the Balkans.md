# Second "Crusade"

The second [[Ottomans|ottoman]] war is coming up. He no longer has Tunis as an ally. Since Tunis was the crusade target, we will miss out on the Crusade [[Combat]] bonuses.

[[Qara Qoyunlu]] is looking strong with 64K troops and willing to ally me, so I'll curry favours until I can call them in to make the war a lot easier. Egypt is strong as well but they have a truce remaining. Also, we want to avoid allying Egypt as conquering them is vital to rush towards the Middle East and India as soon as possible.

[[Great Britain]] formed, turned [[Anglican]], and since I kept relations high and curried favors in the meantime, I am able to ally them again to call them in against the [[Ottomans|ottoman]].

I plan to park a lot of troops in [[Constantinople]] and immediately cross the strait before his ships can block me. If we manage to occupy the other side they will not be able to block our crossing. If this fails, I plan to keep a big stack around [[Constantinople]] and move another big stack all the way around to siege down a province.

# Circumstances Change

Suddenly I see [[Ottomans]] declaring on Fadl, meaning they have to fight [[Egypt]] too. This is good news since it drags away focus from the Balkans.

Another popup indicates that [[Sweden]] will declare on Denmark soon, meaning that we will likely get a Call to Arms. This may draw our subjects' troops away to the north, meaning that we will have to do much more ourselves. Favours with [[Qara Qoyunlu]] are not ready yet but we can call them in later.

# Wartime

[[Sweden]] declares on Denmark, [[Brandenburg]] and a very weak Norway. Six months later our truce with [[Ottomans]] expires and we can engage. Our troops immediately cross the strait, safeguarding it for the rest of the war. As expected, this results in the occupation of entire Balkans without a fight.

![[2012EF~1.jpg]]

The [[Ottomans]] have 130K troops. Our allies (and subjects) are partially distracted in northern Europe, meaning we are not very dominant in Anatolia and we have to play very slow. As soon as the [[Ottomans]]' focus switches towards Egypt we siege down multiple forts.

The [[Ottomans]] peace out Egypt and vassalize [[Fadl]], who has [[Religious Ideas]] and [[Administrative Ideas]], which would make them a very good subject for us, too.

However we are able to blitz siege much of his land, enough for the War Score to reach levels where we can demand a 100% peace deal.

# Peace Deal

![[2066AA~1.jpg]]

We cede the War Goal to [[Byzantium]] and retake all their cores. We create an enclave of [[Ottomans|ottoman]] Balkans containing Bulgarian cores, creating an opportunity for Bulgarian Rebels to give us free land.

We still include maximum money and war reparations which can help us embrace Colonialism, setting up our economy and funding our war effort.

We take a province next to Egypt to be able to make further progress to the southeast. I regret not picking [[Religious Ideas]] now, which would enable a holy war against Egypt making our expansion a lot easier, especially since the military part has not been as tough as I anticipated.
